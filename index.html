Frederik Hahn

Quantum Information Researcher

I research quantum information theory at the intersection of physics, computer science, and mathematics, specializing in quantum communication and quantum cryptography. Currently a postdoctoral fellow at Technische Universität Berlin, I completed my Ph.D. at Freie Universität Berlin, and studied physics and mathematics at Leibniz Universität Hannover and at Cornell University in Ithaca, New York.

About Me

My research revolves around communication in quantum networks and quantum cryptography, investigating how quantum information can provide secure — and occasionally anonymous — communication. I'm particularly interested in developing quantum communication protocols, efficient entanglement routing in networks, and the rich world of quantum graph states.

Research Interests

  • Quantum Networks & Communication
  • Quantum Cryptography
  • Graph State Theory
